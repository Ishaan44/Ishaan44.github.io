Ishaan Bhadoo

I am a Master’s student (Part III of the Mathematical Tripos) in Mathematics at Trinity College, University of Cambridge. My primary research interest is in probability theory.

This fall, I will begin my PhD at Cambridge under the supervision of Prof. Wendelin Werner.

You can reach me at ishaanbhadoo@gmail.com or ib530@cam.ac.uk.

Research

  • Percolation of Words — Ongoing work (joint with Ritvik Radhakrishnan) on the problem of percolation of words.
    • We generalised a result of Kesten–Benjamini for periodic trees, proving that all words appear in a locally finite tree. [PDF]
    • We resolved the truncation problem in the non-amenable setting. [Details, pp. 6–7]
    • Progress summary: [Draft]
  • Critical Threshold for Regular Graphs — [arXiv:2412.00635]

Writings

  • Percolation Thresholds under Coverings — A study of percolation thresholds under graph coverings and the use of enhancement techniques and interpolation schemes. This approach shows that trees uniquely minimise the critical point among quasi-transitive regular graphs.
    • Presentation at TIFR: [Slides]
    • Paper: [arXiv]
  • Percolation on Hyperbolic Graphs — A survey following Tom Hutchcroft’s proof of the uniqueness conjecture in the hyperbolic setting [Paper].
    • [Report] [Slides, ICTS]

  • Aizenman–Kesten–Newman Uniqueness — An exposition of the AKN proof of uniqueness of the infinite cluster for amenable graphs. [PDF]

  • Biautomaticity of Coxeter Groups — Notes from a conference on Geometry in Groups at ICTS, Bangalore (July–August 2023). [PDF]

  • Percolation Theory (MStat, ISI Delhi) — Lecture notes for Prof. Rahul Roy’s course. [Notes]
